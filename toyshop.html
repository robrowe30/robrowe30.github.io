The Magic Toy Shop

This project was to design an e-commerce website for The Magic Toy Shop. A store selling retro and new toys looking to be the big brand with the little store feel. The Final solution was a site that used large images of the toys spread across the whole width of the page with simple navigation at the top and a fun sense of branding.

Brief

The project was to design a e-commerce website proposal for a brand called The Magic Toy Shop. The site had to be both intuitive for its users and clearly present the companies brand values. The Magic Toy Shop was a brand that sold both new and retro toys and wanted to show it valued its unique catalog of offerings coming across like a small boutique while selling a large number of products, the big brand with the little store feel.

Personas

We were provided with three personas for this project; John - The dad looking for quality products to meet his daughters ever changing interests, Tamsin - After magic gifts for her grandson and an easy stress free experience and Dexter - The tech savy collector after the next quality product he can proudly show off. Full versions of these personas were used to make sure design decisions made reflected the people that would use the site.

Competitive Analysis

It was important to both see how other Toy companies worked to see where the Magic Toy Shop fitted in. Big toy brands such as Toys R Us were looked at and had websites that appeared cluttered and overwhelming but offered everything. Boutiques such as Kid Robot showed us a cleaner sites with more emphasis on product quality but often felt limited. Also other e-commerce sites such as John Lewisâ€™s toy section showed us very usable interfaces for large product ranges but more bland branding global to the whole site. We felt our brand required the usability of John Lewis with the approachable feel of the boutique stores.

Card Sorting

The Magic Toy shop had a wide variety of different product and to understand how to put these into the most effective categories I carried out a number of card sorts, firstly with physical cards and then online letting a larger number of people sort through products watching what categories people thought most appropriate for products. This was important as I wanted to avoid the confusion of stores such as The Entertainer and Toys R Us.

User Flows and Site Map

In addition to card sorting to ensure the sites navigation was fluid I created user flows for both finding and purchasing products on an e-commerce site. This helped me to see the process required for The Magic Toy shop.In addition I created a site map to start to provide structure to decisions made during the card sort and from the user flows.

Sketching

Design for the sites interface started with sketching to quickly develop a range of ideas that could be narrowed down. Based on research the design emphasis was to allow for large pictures of the toys uncluttered by unnecessary information while at the same time providing a navigation format that allowed users to easily find desired products.

Wireframes

Once a design had been narrowed down I increased the fidelity creating more realistic wireframes using Omnigraffle. This also made it easier to make designs across different pages more uniform and gave a better understanding of the scale of content required for a web page.

User Testing

Once wireframes had reached an appropriate fidelity, pages for specific user flows were loaded into Marvel App as a clickable prototype. This meant I could then sit down with various users to test the website in a more realistic fashion. This lead to alterations to navigation and some of the language used in the checkout process particularly.

Final Design

The final gives priority to large images letting customers clearly see exactly what they are getting. This also allows for faster browsing with users quickly identifying products. The feel of the site is inviting only displaying important information and using friendly language to help users enjoy the brand, a key requirement of the brief and personas.

See below an overview of key pages and a clickable demo at the bottom.

Home Page

This contains the top nav that remains persistent throughout the site. The brand is clearly displayed at the beginning and the page is not overloaded with information. Primarily the user can navigate to the top nav or view one of the stores personalised collections at the bottom.

Category Page

Products allowed to cover the full width of the page as all filters appear under the top nav. Additionally a zoom control is added to the top nav to allow users to make product images bigger all smaller, so personalising their experience.

Product Page

Product information is clearly displayed and a variety of images available. Recommended products can also be seen below. Above the top nav basket information and contact details are easily viewable (persistent on every page).

Checkout Page

Friendly language is used and progress through checkout is easily viewable via top progress bar enhanced with brand stars, all to improve experience at an important end stage for the user. Entered information is also simple and clearly titled at each stage.
